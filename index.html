Matthew Hur - Graduate Student Researcher

About Me

I am a PhD student at UC Irvine in the Mathematical, Computational, and Systems Biology program. For my research projects, I am working on simulating systems in neuroscience. Previously, I have interned in labs working on electrophysiology and imaging of bone processes, and my work experience includes bioinformatics at Yale Cancer Center.

Research

  • Coded analysis algorithms applied to electrophysiology including the Robust Gaussian Surprise (RGS) burst/pause detection and Welch's method for frequency-spectral enhancement for stationary waveforms.
    • RGS and Welch Analysis in Addiction
    • RGS in Parkinson's Disease
  • Coded a fast method to segment all vertebrae in 3D of zebrafish microCT images. Pipeline is named FishCuT.
    • FishCuT Helps Validate Zebrafish as a Model Organism for Bone Disorders
  • Performed bioinformatics applied to ChIPseq and RNAseq in a tumor suppression system related to B-Cell Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia.
    • Potential Treatment Target for B-Cell ALL
